1. Model Overview

The latent-liquidity project detects and forecasts financial crises by inferring a hidden liquidity factor that drives volatility, correlations, and credit risk. A state-space framework captures the temporal evolution of these hidden drivers while linking them to observed market data, enabling both filtering (past/present) and forecasting (future) within a coherent probabilistic model.

  • Goal: estimate latent market stress and produce probabilistic crisis warnings.
  • Why SSM: hidden liquidity conditions are unobserved; state-space models naturally separate latent dynamics (state) from noisy measurements (observations) and support sequential Bayesian updates.

2. State-Space Model Structure

Latent State Vector

We define the latent state as:

$$ x_t = [L_t,\, h_t,\, z_t] $$

  • Lt: latent liquidity / systemic stress factor
  • ht: log-volatility, so variance is $$\sigma_t^2 = e^{h_t}$$
  • zt: latent correlation driver, $$\rho_t = \tanh(z_t)$$ ensures correlations stay in [-1,1]

Transition Dynamics

Latent states evolve according to autoregressive stochastic dynamics:

$$ \begin{aligned} L_{t+1} &= \phi_L L_t + \beta_L e^{h_t} + \epsilon_L,\\ h_{t+1} &= \mu_h + \phi_h (h_t - \mu_h) + \epsilon_h,\\ z_{t+1} &= \phi_z z_t + \gamma L_t + \epsilon_z, \end{aligned} $$

Process noises $$\epsilon_L, \epsilon_h, \epsilon_z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$$ allow stochastic evolution and capture regime shifts.

Observation Model

Observables:

$$ y_t = [r_{SPY}, r_{TLT}, r_{HYG}, VIX, Spread, Corr] $$

  • Returns: $$ r_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \Sigma_t), \quad \Sigma_t = e^{h_t} R(\rho_t) $$, where R is an equicorrelation matrix.
  • VIX: $$ VIX_t \sim \mathcal{N}(a_{vix} + b_{vix} e^{h_t/2}, \sigma_{vix}^2) $$
  • Credit Spread: $$ Spread_t \sim \mathcal{N}(a_{sp} + b_{sp} L_t, \sigma_{sp}^2) $$
  • Rolling Correlation: $$ Corr_t \sim \mathcal{N}(a_{corr} + b_{corr} \rho_t, \sigma_{corr}^2) $$

3. Data Inputs

  • SPY returns: equity risk proxy
  • TLT returns: flight-to-quality / rates proxy
  • HYG returns: credit risk & funding stress
  • VIX: implied volatility linked to $$h_t$$
  • Credit Spreads: widen as liquidity deteriorates $$L_t$$
  • Rolling Correlations: capture market co-movement driven by $$\rho_t$$

4. Inference Method

Latent states are inferred using particle filtering (Sequential Monte Carlo), approximating the posterior:

$$ p(x_t \mid y_{1:t}) \approx \sum_i w_t^{(i)} \delta(x_t - x_t^{(i)}) $$

  1. Prediction: propagate particles through the transition model.
  2. Weighting: compute likelihood $$w_t^{(i)} \propto p(y_t \mid x_t^{(i)})$$.
  3. Resampling: focus on high-weight particles to avoid degeneracy.

Posterior summaries (means, quantiles) of $$L_t, h_t, z_t$$ are saved to results/crisis_res.npz and displayed in the dashboard.

5. Crisis Prediction Methodology

  1. Sample particles from the current posterior.
  2. Simulate forward using the transition model over forecast horizon.
  3. Compute crisis risk metrics, e.g. $$P(L_{t+h} > L_{crit})$$, tail probabilities, or composite risk signals.

These forward simulations produce probabilistic crisis paths and visual risk cones in the dashboard.

6. Interpretation

  • Liquidity spirals: rising $$L_t$$ signals funding pressure and spread widening.
  • Volatility clustering: persistent high $$h_t$$ captures risk-on/off cycles.
  • Correlation breakdown: high $$\rho_t$$ reflects market co-movement during stress.

Jointly, these mechanisms provide early warnings of systemic instability across market regimes.
