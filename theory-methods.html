1. Model Overview

The latent-liquidity project detects and forecasts financial crises by inferring a hidden liquidity factor that drives volatility, correlations, and credit risk. A state-space framework captures the temporal evolution of these hidden drivers while linking them to observed market data, enabling filtering (past/present) and forecasting (future) within one coherent probabilistic model.

  • Goal: estimate latent market stress and produce probabilistic crisis warnings.
  • Why SSM: hidden liquidity conditions are not directly observed; SSMs naturally separate latent dynamics (state) from noisy measurements (observations) and support sequential Bayesian updates.

2. State-Space Model Structure

Latent state vector

x_t = [L_t,\ h_t,\ z_t]

  • L_t: latent liquidity / systemic stress factor.
  • h_t: log-volatility (so variance is \sigma_t^2 = e^{h_t}).
  • z_t: latent correlation driver (\rho_t = \tanh(z_t) keeps correlations in [-1,1]).

State transitions

\begin{aligned} L_{t+1} &= \phi_L L_t + \beta_L e^{h_t} + \epsilon_L,\\ h_{t+1} &= \mu_h + \phi_h (h_t - \mu_h) + \epsilon_h,\\ z_{t+1} &= \phi_z z_t + \gamma L_t + \epsilon_z, \end{aligned}

Process noises \epsilon_L, \epsilon_h, \epsilon_z \sim \mathcal N(0, \Sigma_Q) allow stochastic evolution and capture regime shifts.

Observation model

Observables y_t = [r_{SPY}, r_{TLT}, r_{HYG}, VIX, Spread, Corr] are linked to the latent states:

  • Returns: r_t \sim \mathcal N(0, \Sigma_t) with \Sigma_t = e^{h_t} R(\rho_t); R(\rho_t) is an equicorrelation matrix driven by \rho_t = \tanh(z_t).
  • VIX: VIX_t \sim \mathcal N(a_{vix} + b_{vix} e^{h_t/2}, \sigma_{vix}^2).
  • Credit Spread: Spread_t \sim \mathcal N(a_{sp} + b_{sp} L_t, \sigma_{sp}^2).
  • Rolling Corr: Corr_t \sim \mathcal N(a_{corr} + b_{corr} \rho_t, \sigma_{corr}^2).

3. Data Inputs

  • SPY returns: equity risk proxy.
  • TLT returns: rates/liquidity flight-to-quality proxy.
  • HYG returns: credit risk and funding stress.
  • VIX: implied volatility linked to h_t.
  • Credit spreads: widen as liquidity deteriorates (L_t).
  • Rolling correlations: capture market co-movement driven by \rho_t.

4. Inference Method

The latent states are inferred with particle filtering (Sequential Monte Carlo), approximating p(x_t \mid y_{1:t}) by weighted particles.

  1. Prediction: propagate particles via transition model.
  2. Weighting: compute likelihood w_t^{(i)} \propto p(y_t \mid x_t^{(i)}).
  3. Resampling: focus on high-weight particles to avoid degeneracy.

Posterior summaries (means, quantiles) of L_t, h_t, z_t are saved to results/crisis_res.npz and surfaced in the dashboard.

5. Crisis Prediction Methodology

Based on crisis_predictor.py, forward simulations use the filtered state as a starting distribution:

  1. Sample particles from current posterior.
  2. Simulate transitions forward over the forecast horizon.
  3. Compute crisis risk metrics, e.g. P(L_{t+h} > L_{crit}), tail probabilities, or composite risk signals.

These simulations produce crisis probability paths and risk cones visualized in the dashboard/animations.

6. Interpretation

  • Liquidity spirals: rising L_t signals funding pressure and spread widening.
  • Volatility clustering: persistent high h_t captures risk-on/off cycles.
  • Correlation breakdown: high \rho_t reflects market co-movement during stress.

Jointly, these mechanisms provide early warnings of systemic instability and support crisis monitoring across regimes.
